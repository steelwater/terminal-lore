# Terminal Technology

Overview of the technologies surrounding the Terminal program.

## Contextual Phase Adaptation (CPA)

CPA allows a Terminal to adapt its appearance to match the technological
expectations of a specific era.

Rather than continuous transformation, the Terminal performs a
contextual alignment when discovered.

Example expressions: - Ancient eras: sacred artifact or relic -
Industrial era: mechanical instrument - Modern era: computer terminal -
Post‑collapse era: handheld control device

CPA reduces suspicion while maintaining full functionality.

## Temporal Capsules

Temporal capsules are containment systems used to send Terminals into
earlier historical periods.

Functions: - Chronolith Resonant reactor - Temporal insertion
stabilization - Environmental protection for centuries

Capsules are typically hidden inside anomalous zones where spacetime
instability allows temporal displacement.

## Warp Systems

Human warp travel relies on Chronolith Residue.

Ships do not move faster than light in the conventional sense.\
Instead, they move through spacetime distortions created by Chronolith
Prime.

Without Chronolith contamination within the Solar System, warp travel
would be impossible.

## Terminal AI

Two known AI systems:

### Prompt

Associated with Terminal 582.\
Primary directive: prevent discovery of Horrox.

### Vector

Associated with Terminal 1395.\
Designed to counter Prompt's interventions.

Both AI systems interpret directives independently and may conflict.
