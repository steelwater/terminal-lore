# Chronolith Specification

LightTech Temporal Systems Division -- Technical Summary

Chronolith is a rare extraterrestrial mineral compound responsible for
spacetime instability, anomalous zones on Earth, and the enabling of
temporal and warp technologies.

## Chronolith Prime

Origin: Ancient meteor impact (pre-human era)

Characteristics: - Structurally stable crystalline lattice - Inert to
normal chemical reactions - Creates long‑term spacetime stress fields

Effects: - Formation of anomalous zones on Earth - Geological
disturbances - Planetary scale spacetime distortions

Chronolith Prime fractures spacetime but does not actively manipulate
it.

## Chronolith Residue

Origin: Microscopic fragments degraded from Chronolith Prime.

Characteristics: - Chemically inert - Extremely low mass particles -
Reacts with high‑energy spacetime fields

Effects: - Stabilizes warp systems - Enables interstellar transit
through spacetime fractures - Leaves temporal signatures detectable by
advanced sensors

Residue is often transported unknowingly on spacecraft and equipment.

## Chronolith Resonant

Origin: Modern meteor containing an evolved Chronolith compound.

Characteristics: - Highly reactive to energy - Resonates with existing
spacetime fractures - Amplifies anomalous zone activity

Effects: - Enables temporal capsule insertion - Activates dormant
anomalous zones - Allows Contextual Phase Adaptation (CPA) systems to
function

Chronolith Resonant is the primary material used in LightTech temporal
systems.

## Scientific Interpretation

Chronolith behaves like a spacetime catalyst. It alters the topology of
space rather than serving as a traditional energy source.

Its origin remains unknown.
